# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single person using an Arch Linux and Hyprland desktop during focused work. They need to understand the timer at a glance, operate it without breaking concentration, and trust it to behave predictably throughout a Timer Run.

## Product Purpose

Guide alternating Focus Sessions and Breaks through a compact, local desktop utility. Success means the current interval, remaining time, progression context, and available action are always immediately clear without turning the experience into task management or productivity measurement.

## Brand Personality

Modern, calm, minimal. The Pomodoro Timer should feel like a quiet and dependable desktop instrument rather than a motivational system competing for attention.

## Anti-references

Avoid gamified tomato imagery, streaks, rewards, achievement mechanics, celebratory productivity theatre, and analytics-dashboard styling. Avoid visual noise or ornamental effects that make the timer harder to read or feel less trustworthy.

## Design Principles

1. **Make time glanceable.** Remaining time and the current interval must be understandable without deliberate inspection.
2. **Make state unmistakable.** The interface should communicate what is happening and what action is available without relying on memory or inference.
3. **Protect concentration.** Every element and feedback moment should earn the attention it consumes.
4. **Show useful context, not performance.** Explain the current Focus Session and Pomodoro Cycle position without implying goals, scores, or analytics.
5. **Earn desktop trust.** Familiar controls, predictable behavior, and restrained feedback matter more than novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA. Support complete keyboard operation, visible focus, reduced-motion preferences, sufficient text and control contrast, and state cues that do not rely on color alone. Completion Feedback should remain informative without stealing focus.
